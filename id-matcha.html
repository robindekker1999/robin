A story about matcha:
chaos and calmness

Telling the story about the origin of matcha in a visual way through sketching and design. How did the preparation of matcha change from a ceremony to the busy cafés we know today?

  • Information design
  • Storytelling
  • ProCreate
  • Sketching

Gaining knowledge on the subject and turning it into a story

As part of my minor in Information Design, I designed an interactive story exploring the rich history and cultural significance of matcha. To gain firsthand insights, I interviewed the owner of CHUN Café in Amsterdam, a local expert on matcha. This conversation deepened my understanding of matcha's contemporary uses and cultural relevance.

I also researched where matcha comes from, learning about its role in traditional Japanese tea ceremonies and how it became popular around the world as a health trend. By looking at how people have used and thought about matcha over time, I created a story with visuals and interactive elements to help others learn about and enjoy the journey of matcha.

The main challenge with this project is turning all the information about matcha into a digital story with the use of graphics, design and motion.

Exploring different drawing styles and brushes in ProCreate

It’s important to me that the visual style matches the story’s content. I spent a lot of time exploring different styles to achieve this. I researched various Japanese art forms, such as Ukiyo-e woodblock prints, Sumi-e brushstrokes, anime, and minimalist design and architecture. This research helped me understand different design possibilities and guided me in choosing the style that best fit the project and the story.

The main art style for this project is derived from the Japanse style of calligraphy, better known as Sumi-e. To transform this into sketches and visuals, I tried several brushes and styles using ProCreate.

Incorporating the art style into every aspect of the storytelling

After deciding on a style, I looked for ways to incorporate this into different aspects of the design, such as graphics, typogaphy and other small design elements. I wanted to create this feeling of the story being written and drawn with calligraphy brushes on scrolls.

All of the graphics are drawn by me using ProCreate and a lot of style references. Also for the typography of titles and other important element I chose to use my own handwriting to create this authentic feel.

Lastly, I also made a graphic in the Ukiyo-e woodblock printing style, to kind of break the pattern but still keep te Japanese history art style vibe.
